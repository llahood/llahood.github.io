I lead creative teams through breakthrough campaigns and rebrands, specializing in big-picture thinking that makes brands unforgettable.

My approach: create an environment where wild ideas are welcome, collaboration feels natural, and the work reflects the energy we bring to it. Whether it's a national campaign, a complete brand identity, or a digital experience, I thrive at the sweet spot where strategy meets craft.

For over a decade I've been the Senior Art Director at the US Holocaust Memorial Museum in Washington, DC, leading the creative team behind its campaigns, publications, and brand, where the stakes for getting the story right could not be higher. Before that: building brands for a print-on-demand startup through its acquisition, developing a 13,000-piece licensed art collection with global artists, and designing for toys, publishing, and national campaigns.

I was also early to bring AI into the creative process, using it to explore more concepts, move faster, and raise the bar on the finished work rather than lower it.

When I'm not leading creative teams, you'll find me on stage singing the blues, behind a drum kit, or making colorful illustrated prints and tees. Turns out I can't stop making things, even when I try.
